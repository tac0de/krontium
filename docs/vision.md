# Krontium Vision

## Core Premise

Krontium is a grounded epic fantasy built around three ancient gods whose forms echo biological history:

- Earth god: a tree-human form shaped by roots, bark, fungi, early land ecology, and deep soil memory.
- Sky god: a bird-human form shaped by feathered anatomy, flight, bone structure, and traces of dinosaur-to-bird evolution.
- Sea god: an octopus-human form shaped by cephalopod intelligence, chromatophores, soft anatomy, and deep marine life.

Humans are physically weak compared with older beings and other peoples, but they advance quickly through tools, writing, coordination, institutions, and military adaptation.

Between hostile powers sits a neutral monastic zone. Its monks preserve chronicles, specimens, genealogies, war testimony, ecological observations, and theological disputes.

## Tone

Krontium should feel more like a recovered historical archive than a decorative fantasy website.

Use:

- monastic record rooms
- biological plates and specimen logic
- war maps and campaign ledgers
- weathered bronze, ink, stone, bark, bone, salt, and vellum
- restrained sacred atmosphere

Avoid:

- generic magical glow
- game boss framing
- cute fantasy ornament
- pure marketing-page composition
- lore dumps without historical texture

## Website Experience

The first screen is the archive itself. Language selection, world fragments, and reading paths should appear as functional archival controls rather than promotional calls to action.

The reader experience should be calm, legible, and serious. Interactive world data should support reading without overwhelming the prose.
